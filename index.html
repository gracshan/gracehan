Network Traffic Engineering

Description:

Network traffic engineering, aimed at enhancing performance metrics like throughput, fairness, and latency, was achieved through the development of routing mechanisms in the SDN framework. Notably, SDN's distinctive feature of separating the control and data planes was leveraged to create an abstracted centralized view for routing decisions. The linear program formulation employed decision variables, constraints, and objectives, capturing the complexity of SDN traffic engineering by addressing performance enhancement and network resource limitations.

The algorithms that were focused on minimizing the maximum link usage demonstrated an ability to distribute network traffic more evenly, proving advantageous in situations where the consequences of lost packets were substantial. Conversely, the strategy of maximizing throughput was identified as the most efficient approach for network performance. However, it was observed that link failures, particularly on high-traffic edges, had significant and potentially detrimental effects on the overall stability and functionality of the network. Therefore, a nuanced consideration of the trade-offs between achieving even distribution and optimizing overall efficiency was crucial in the design and implementation of the software-defined networking solutions.

Used: Python, NetworkX, Gurobi

Feature Detector

Description:

A feature detector is a computational algorithm designed to identify distinctive patterns, structures, or points within an image that can be used to describe and distinguish objects or regions. These detectors play a crucial role in image processing applications, extracting meaningful information from images to facilitate tasks such as object recognition, image stitching, and tracking. I identified key points or regions in each image, such as corners or keypoints, that exhibit unique characteristics and are invariant to transformations like scaling, rotation, and changes in illumination.

To further enhance the accuracy of feature matching, I constructed descriptors for each identified point. These descriptors encapsulated essential features, facilitating a robust comparison between corresponding points in different images. This comprehensive approach aimed to ensure precise alignment and stitching of images for the creation of seamless panoramas. As a crucial component of the project, I also implemented a graphical user interface (GUI) to visualize the matches between descriptors. This GUI provided an intuitive representation of how distinctive points aligned across images, enhancing the interpretability of the matching process.

Used: Python, NumPy, SciPy, OpenCv

Flight Search

Description:

In crafting a comprehensive flight search application, I incorporated a range of functionalities to cater to users' diverse travel needs. The application empowered users to precisely specify their travel criteria, encompassing not only the origin and destination but also the flexibility to input preferred travel dates, currency choices, and sorting preferences. This granular level of customization allowed users to tailor their search queries according to their unique travel requirements. The emphasis on user-friendly design principles and a well-organized presentation of flight options aimed to enhance the overall usability and efficiency of the application, empowering users to make informed decisions about their travel arrangements.

The user interface (UI) of the application was designed to adhere to intuitive principles. The search results were displayed in a tabular format, presenting a detailed overview of available flights, including vital information such as departure and arrival times, durations, layovers, and, importantly, pricing details. To enhance user decision-making, the application strategically highlighted the most cost-effective flight options, ensuring that users could quickly identify the most budget-friendly choices. Furthermore, I incorporated additional features such as filters, allowing users to refine their search results based on specific preferences like direct flights or preferred airlines.

Used: React, HTML/CSS/JS, TypeScript
